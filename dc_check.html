Does DC make sense
for this facility?

Five questions to check whether a site is worth a deeper electrical architecture review.

Progress Question 1 of 5
Question 1 — Scale
What is this facility's approximate annual electricity bill?
A rough estimate is fine. This is the single biggest indicator of whether optimisation makes economic sense.
Question 2 — Equipment
Which of these are running in the facility?
Select all that apply. The more categories present, the stronger the case for a DC architecture.
Robots & Precision Machines
Industrial robots (welding, pick-and-place) · CNC machines · Servo-drive machinery
Automated Material Movement
Conveyor belts & sorters · Automated storage systems · AGVs / autonomous mobile robots
Electrified Equipment & Charging
Electric forklifts & pallet jacks · EV / fleet chargers · Battery storage systems (UPS, BESS)
Electronic Motors & Facility Systems
Pumps, fans, compressors (with variable speed drive) · HVAC systems with modern controls · LED lighting, sensors, control panels
None of the above
Question 3 — Renewables
Does the facility have solar panels or battery storage — or plans to install them?
Solar and batteries are DC by nature. When they're present, every conversion step between source and load is a direct loss opportunity.
Question 4 — Timing
Is the facility planning any of the following in the next two years?
Select all that apply. A planned change creates the ideal window for architectural improvements. It's far cheaper than retrofitting later.
Plant expansion or relocation
New production line or major equipment purchase
Electrical or energy infrastructure upgrade
Greenfield or new build project
Nothing planned right now
Question 5 — Direction
Is the facility replacing gas processes with electric, or rolling out electric vehicles on site?
Electrification increases total electrical load, and usually adds more DC-native equipment. This amplifies the long-term benefit of a DC architecture.
